Cave Volume Estimation

Research Project 2025 3D Geometry

Overview

This project implements a method to calculate cave passage volumes from survey data using truncated cone approximation with intersection correction. The system processes compass and clinometer measurements to create 3D representations of cave passages and accurately calculates their volumes while accounting for overlapping segments at junctions.

Method

The volume calculation follows a three-step process:

  1. Survey Processing: Convert compass/clino/length measurements to 3D coordinates
  2. Volume Calculation: Approximate each passage segment as a truncated elliptical cone
  3. Intersection Correction: Use voxel grid to detect and subtract overlapping volumes at junctions

Survey Processing

Measurements contain information on distance to the top, bottom, left, and right walls of a cave section from measurement points. Length of the segment, inclination, and orientation are measured for the section of cave between the current and next measurement point.

Point locations are calculated from section length, compass (yaw around Z-axis), and clinometer reading (pitch around Y-axis). The first point in the data is read as (0,0,0).

The coordinate transformations are:

  • dx = LENGTH · cos(CLINO) · sin(90 - COMPASS)
  • dy = LENGTH · cos(CLINO) · cos(90 - COMPASS)
  • dz = LENGTH · sin(CLINO)

Volume Formula

For each segment, the volume is calculated using the truncated cone formula:

V = (π/3) · L · (A1 + A2 + √(A1 · A2))

Where:

  • L = segment length
  • A1 = width1 × height1 (start cross-section area)
  • A2 = width2 × height2 (end cross-section area)

Intersection Correction

As the method of measuring can lead to overlapping segments, volume corrections are necessary to remove potential redundant volumes. Cave segments are considered as their outer surfaces and represented parametrically.

The parametric representation of a truncated elliptical cone is defined by interpolating between the start and end ellipses, with proper orientation in 3D space determined by rotation matrices around the x-axis (polar orientation) and y-axis (inclination).

The segments are then voxelized, shared voxels are considered as overlap, and removed once to prevent double-counting of volumes at intersections.

Data Requirements

The system processes cave survey data in CSV format with the following required columns:

Column Description Unit
from Start station name -
to End station name -
length Distance meters
compass Bearing degrees
clino Inclination degrees
left, right, up, down Passage dimensions meters

Key Features

  • 3D reconstruction of cave passages from survey measurements
  • Accurate volume calculation using truncated elliptical cone approximation
  • Intersection detection and correction using voxelization
  • Visualization tools for cave geometry and cross-sections
  • Support for complex cave systems with multiple junctions
